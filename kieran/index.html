Projects

Antihydrogen at CERN

For my MPhys dissertation I spent a semester working with the ALPHA collaboration at CERN in Geneva. ALPHA study the matter – antimatter asymmetry problem by for example, looking at the 1S2S transition in antihydrogen and comparing it to the hydrogen equivalent. A 243nm laser is used to excite antihydrogen from the 1S energy state to the 2S energy state, where the energy difference can be studied. My job was to measure and aid with improvements in the linewidth of this laser so that this energy difference can be known to a greater precision.

Faulkes Telescope Project

I utilized numerous robotic telescopes across the world for an observation and analyzation program I developed for sky objects, enhancing my writing and data gathering abilities. The process involved
  • Scheduling observation slots weeks in advance based on the telescope location, sky object coordinates, aperture size
  • Rescheduling under pressure when factors such as the weather reducing visibility prevented the image being taken
  • Analysing characteristics from successful images to determine properties of the objects
  • Producing a journal and presenting a poster upon the drawn conclusion of the project
